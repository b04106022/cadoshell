Avo's Tech News

     More and more chatbots have popped out on Facebook, LINE, or some other websites these days, giving online users recommendations or advices for foods, trips, and sometimes even health problems.  Some chatbots also provide customer services and collect their opinions meanwhile.  What’s behind the chatbots is the so-called Conversational AI Platform Technology, which has gathered both linguistics and computational scientists together to work hard over the past few years.


     Sounding distant, though, Conversational AI platform technology is actually developed with the intention that users can use a variety of natural languages, such as voice, text, or gesture etc., to interact and communicate with the device or application.  And all kinds of online chat boxes have been widely adopted by enterprises to provide customers with online services or as a customer survey tool.


     It’s assumed that by the year of 2020, most of the relationships between customers and enterprises will be preferred not to built on the interaction with humans.  That’s probably when chatbots start to take over the customer-service stuff; by the time, humans no longer need to tolerate other humans.  However, improving the ways how chatbots understand users’ need and how they answer remains the main topic in this field.  With human intervention, say users’ or developers’ touch or other feedbacks, it can speed up the process of machine learning and help a lot to increase the degree of accuracy with which the chatbots act more natural and humanlike.
